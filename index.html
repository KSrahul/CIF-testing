Founded in 2018, the China India Foundation is a non-profit organization. Our mission is to increase mutual understanding between the people of India and China by creating avenues for collaboration, building people-to-people exchange, and strengthening the capacity for cross-cultural engagement.

Why Now

Historical Ties

India and China are two of the oldest continuous civilizations that share a rich history of people-to-people and cultural exchange. Apart from trade and Buddhism, both countries exchanged knowledge in areas such as mathematics, astronomy, and medicine, dating back to 200 BCE. However, relations between the two countries from the mid-20th century onwards have been strained, and exchange has been sparse.

Fastest Growing Major Economies

Today, China is the world’s second largest economy, and India is the world’s 6th largest economy. Trade between the two countries hit a record high of US$84.4 billion in 2017, growing at double-digit growth rates despite bilateral tensions. In the 21st century, China and India’s growth will be led by their digital economies and will continue to become more interdependent. China has pledged to expand its $3.8 trillion digital economy and create jobs in new sectors like big data and artificial intelligence. India’s digital economy is expected to increase three times over a ten-year period to $1.15 trillion.

Our history of exchange of wisdom and shared future of technological and economic growth gives our organization a strong foundation and context on which to build and deepen people-to-people understanding.

Emerging Global Influence

The partnership between China and India will arguably become one of the most important partnerships of the 21st century. By 2030, led by China and India, Asia will surpass the combined power of North America and Europe in terms of economic influence. With their massive populations, economies, and environmental impacts, these two countries have an outsized effect on the trajectory of global affairs. It is therefore crucial that these two countries can foster a healthy working relationship on bilateral, regional, and global issues. Increasing citizen-level mutual understanding is a vital part of this process.

Our Approach

Education

We will work with a consortium of universities to implement best practices to increase student exchange between the two countries. We will also work with institutions in India to develop China Studies as a discipline, creating the infrastructure for in-depth analysis and understanding.

Cultural Exchange

We will leverage new media to tell human stories, sharing how people live their daily lives and celebrating the everyday cultures of India and China.

Commercial Exchange

We will act as a facilitator to help Chinese and Indian businesses understand each other better. Given the growth in the technology sector and increasing tech investment, we will specifically focus on facilitating understanding for technology and new media companies about each country’s landscape, market, and business culture. Lastly, given the increasingly global role of Chinese and Indian companies, we will increase partnerships and exchange on global and local management practices, focusing specifically on both countries’ management institutions.

Issue-Based Collaborations

We will facilitate practitioner exchange and knowledge partnerships on mutually beneficial policy areas. The areas we are currently exploring are environmental challenges, vocational training, and agricultural reform.

Timeline

Our Team

Ashish Dhawan
Founder

Ashish Dhawan is an Indian private equity investor and philanthropist who founded one of India's leading private equity funds, Chrys Capital. He left his position at Chrys Capital in 2012 to found Central Square Foundation (CSF), a non-profit foundation working towards quality K12 education for all children in India. In 2014, he founded India's first liberal arts university, Ashoka University, and is the Chairman of the University’s Board. Ashish has also launched the Indian School of Development Management, an institution focused on creating development managers for the social sector. Ashish graduated from Yale University and received his MBA from Harvard Business School. Ashish is currently the Chair of Harvard’s India Research Center, is on the Global Advisory Board for Harvard Business School, and is a Sterling Fellow at Yale University.

Anushka Prasad
Founding Director

Anushka Prasad graduated as a Schwarzman Scholar from Tsinghua University, Beijing. The Schwarzman Scholars program, founded by Steve Schwarzman, CEO of Blackstone, is the first program to respond to the geopolitical landscape of the 21st Century and aims to equip scholars with a deep understanding of China’s role in global trends. Prior to Schwarzman, Anushka worked with Ashish Dhawan at Ashoka University leading strategic initiatives building Ashoka's governance structures, government relations, and university policy. Anushka has also worked at Swiss Re in New York on their strategy team, as a part of the CEO of North America’s office. She graduated from New York University with Highest Honors in Economics and Psychology.

Suhail S. Thandi

Suhail Thandi is a graduate of Carleton College, a leading liberal arts institution in the United States. At Carleton, Suhail double majored in Cinema & Media Studies and Political Science & International Relations, graduating with honors in Political Science.
